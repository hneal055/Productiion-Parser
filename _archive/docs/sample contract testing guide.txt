🧪 SAMPLE CONTRACT TESTING GUIDE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

I've created 4 realistic sample contracts with intentional red flags to test
your Contract Review Tool. Here's what each one tests and what the AI should find.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
📄 CONTRACT 1: NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT (NDA)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

File: sample_contract_NDA.txt
Type: Mutual NDA (but actually one-sided)
Difficulty: EASY - Clear red flags

WHAT TO TEST:
✓ Basic term extraction
✓ Identification of one-sided terms
✓ Recognition of unreasonable time periods
✓ Detection of overly broad restrictions

MAJOR RED FLAGS THE AI SHOULD FIND:
1. 10-year term + indefinite extension
   → Industry standard is 2-5 years
   
2. Ownership of ALL work product (even unrelated ideas)
   → Should only cover confidential information-related work
   
3. 5-year non-compete clause
   → Likely unenforceable, extremely broad
   
4. One-sided obligations
   → Only Receiving Party has obligations, not mutual
   
5. Unlimited damages
   → Should have reasonable limitation
   
6. Unilateral amendment rights
   → Only Disclosing Party can amend

EXPECTED AI OUTPUT:
- Risk Level: HIGH
- Fairness: UNFAVORABLE
- Negotiation: Make mutual, reduce term, remove non-compete
- Key Terms: Identify the 10-year term, ownership clause

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
📄 CONTRACT 2: FREELANCE SERVICE AGREEMENT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

File: sample_contract_FREELANCE.txt
Type: Independent contractor agreement
Difficulty: MEDIUM - Multiple subtle issues

WHAT TO TEST:
✓ Payment term analysis
✓ IP ownership detection
✓ Non-compete evaluation
✓ Termination rights comparison

MAJOR RED FLAGS THE AI SHOULD FIND:
1. Net 90 payment terms
   → Industry standard is Net 30, maybe Net 60
   → 3 months is excessive
   
2. One-sided termination
   → Client: 30 days
   → Contractor: 180 days + early termination fee
   
3. IP assignment includes pre-existing work
   → "Prior to execution" clause is a huge red flag
   
4. Cannot use work in portfolio
   → Unreasonable for designer/contractor
   
5. 36-month non-compete
   → Way too long for freelancer
   → Covers entire industries
   
6. Unlimited liability
   → Contractor liable for "any and all damages"
   
7. Automatic renewal with 120-day notice
   → Easy to miss and get locked in
   
8. "Exclusive" work during business hours
   → Conflicts with independent contractor status

EXPECTED AI OUTPUT:
- Risk Level: HIGH
- Fairness: UNFAVORABLE
- Negotiation: Net 30 payment, mutual termination, limit IP to work created during engagement
- Key Terms: Payment terms, termination, IP ownership, non-compete

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
📄 CONTRACT 3: COMMERCIAL LEASE AGREEMENT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

File: sample_contract_LEASE.txt
Type: Commercial retail lease
Difficulty: HARD - Hidden costs throughout

WHAT TO TEST:
✓ Hidden cost identification
✓ Complex fee structure analysis
✓ Liability clause detection
✓ Termination rights imbalance

MAJOR RED FLAGS THE AI SHOULD FIND:
1. Hidden maintenance costs
   → Tenant pays for HVAC, roof, structural repairs
   → Industry standard: landlord covers structure/roof
   → Could be $12,000+ per year
   
2. 8% annual rent increase
   → Way above typical 3-4% or CPI
   
3. Non-refundable security deposit
   → Security deposits should be refundable!
   
4. One-sided termination
   → Landlord: 60 days
   → Tenant: 12 months + ALL remaining rent!
   
5. Unlimited tenant liability
   → Even for landlord's negligence
   
6. 180-day non-renewal notice
   → Easy to miss and auto-renew for 3 years
   
7. Required hours: 7 days/week including holidays
   → Unreasonable for small business
   
8. CAM charges with no cap
   → "Actual costs" can balloon
   
9. Personal guarantee extends beyond lease
   → Owners personally liable forever

EXPECTED AI OUTPUT:
- Risk Level: HIGH
- Fairness: UNFAVORABLE
- Negotiation: Cap maintenance costs, reduce notice period, remove personal guarantee
- Key Terms: All the additional charges, termination terms, liability

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
📄 CONTRACT 4: EMPLOYMENT AGREEMENT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

File: sample_contract_EMPLOYMENT.txt
Type: Software engineer employment contract
Difficulty: HARD - Subtle legal issues

WHAT TO TEST:
✓ IP assignment scope analysis
✓ Non-compete reasonableness
✓ At-will vs. contract conflict detection
✓ Employee rights limitations

MAJOR RED FLAGS THE AI SHOULD FIND:
1. Broad IP assignment
   → Covers ALL inventions, even created at home
   → Should exclude personal projects unrelated to business
   
2. 18-month non-compete
   → Covers entire software industry
   → 100-mile radius + anywhere company does business
   → May be unenforceable in some states (like CA)
   
3. Prior inventions trap
   → Must list or they're deemed assigned to company
   → Easy to forget something
   
4. No PTO payout or carryover
   → "Use it or lose it" policy
   
5. Unilateral modification rights
   → Company can change compensation/benefits anytime
   
6. One-sided arbitration
   → Company picks rules and location
   → Employee pays own fees
   → Class action waiver
   
7. Social media restrictions after employment
   → Overly broad, may limit future opportunities
   
8. At-will employment with restrictive covenants
   → Company can fire anytime but employee can't work elsewhere

EXPECTED AI OUTPUT:
- Risk Level: MEDIUM to HIGH
- Fairness: UNFAVORABLE
- Negotiation: Limit IP to work-related inventions, reduce non-compete, ensure PTO payout
- Key Terms: IP assignment, non-compete, at-will status, arbitration

🐛 WHAT TO LOOK FOR (DEBUGGING)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

GOOD SIGNS:
✓ AI quotes specific clauses from the contract
✓ Risk ratings are mostly HIGH for these contracts
✓ Negotiation points are specific and actionable
